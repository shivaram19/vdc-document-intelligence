# Q1: What Is an Agentic Enterprise, Really?

**Research Question:** What is the smallest, most useful definition of an "agentic enterprise" — and how is it different from automation, a SaaS company, or a traditional team?

---

## Why This Matters for Medha

If we cannot define it simply, we cannot build it. Medha is one human plus AI tools. The agentic enterprise framework must fit that reality, not assume a 100-person company.

---

## Sub-Questions

### 1.1 Definition
1. What is an agentic enterprise at its irreducible core?
2. Is the defining feature autonomous action, tool use, reasoning, or something else?
3. Does an agentic enterprise require multiple agents, or can one agent + one human qualify?
4. What is the difference between an agentic enterprise and an automated enterprise?
5. What is the difference between an agentic enterprise and an AI-augmented startup?

### 1.2 Boundaries
1. Which decisions must always remain human?
2. Which tasks can be fully delegated to agents?
3. At what scale does "agentic" become meaningful — revenue, headcount, agent count, or process coverage?
4. Can a services business be agentic, or only a product/software business?

### 1.3 Historical Precedents
1. What pre-LLM systems were agentic? (e.g., algorithmic trading, robotic process automation, self-healing infrastructure)
2. What did they get right and wrong about autonomy?
3. What can we learn from autonomous organizations in other domains (cooperatives, DAOs, platform cooperatives)?

### 1.4 Minimum Viable Agentic Enterprise (MVAE)
1. What is the smallest agentic enterprise that could plausibly work?
2. What are its essential components?
3. What can be omitted in version 0.1?

---

## Current Hypotheses

1. An agentic enterprise is an organization where **goal-directed agents perform work, use tools, and are accountable through observability**, with humans retaining authority over values, commitments, and exceptions.
2. The smallest viable form is **one human + one orchestrator + specialist agents for each core pipeline**, with human approval gates.
3. The key difference from automation is **adaptivity**: agents choose actions based on context rather than following fixed rules.

---

## What Would Change Our Mind

- Evidence that successful agentic enterprises require minimum team size >1
- Discovery of a widely accepted simpler definition
- Examples of fully autonomous enterprises that are sustainable and ethical

---

## Where to Look for Answers

- arXiv papers on agentic organizations and multi-agent systems
- Essays by founders of AI-native companies (e.g., ProductLed, Build MVP Fast)
- Open-source projects claiming to be "agentic operating systems"
- Philosophy of organizations / future-of-work literature

---

## Medha-Specific Translation

For Medha, an agentic enterprise means:
- Founder sets intent and approves external commitments
- Agents handle drafting, scheduling, monitoring, synthesis, and routine code changes
- Plane remains the system of record
- Every agent action is observable and reversible
