# Q2: How Do Humans Stay Central in an Agentic Enterprise?

**Research Question:** In an organization where agents do much of the work, what is the human's role, and how do we design for human dignity, accountability, and meaningful decision-making?

---

## Why This Matters for Medha

Medha is one human. If agents take over tasks, the founder could become either a supervisor or a spectator. We need a design where the founder remains the author of the company's direction.

---

## Sub-Questions

### 2.1 Human Roles
1. What roles do humans play in the most advanced agentic organizations?
2. Which tasks should humans always own? (e.g., ethics, relationships, capital allocation, hiring, firing)
3. What new human roles emerge? (e.g., agent trainer, governance designer, exception handler)
4. What roles disappear or shrink?

### 2.2 Accountability
1. If an agent makes a bad decision, who is accountable?
2. How do we preserve legal and moral accountability for the human founder?
3. What audit structures make accountability traceable?
4. How do customers and partners know whether they are dealing with a human or an agent?

### 2.3 Dignity and Autonomy
1. Does an agentic enterprise risk deskilling the human?
2. How do we design work so that humans exercise judgment rather than just approving agent outputs?
3. What is the difference between human supervision and human rubber-stamping?
4. How do we prevent "moral crumple zones" where humans are blamed for agent failures?

### 2.4 Human-Agent Collaboration Patterns
1. What patterns work best: human-in-the-loop, human-on-the-loop, or human-out-of-the-loop?
2. When should an agent ask for permission vs. act and report?
3. How do we keep humans meaningfully in control without creating bottlenecks?
4. What UI/UX patterns support good human-agent collaboration?

---

## Current Hypotheses

1. The founder's core roles are **intent-setter, exception-handler, relationship-owner, and final approver**.
2. Agents handle **execution, synthesis, monitoring, and routine decisions** within defined guardrails.
3. Meaningful human control requires **transparent reasoning, veto power, and easy rollback** — not just approval buttons.

---

## What Would Change Our Mind

- Evidence that human-in-the-loop consistently degrades outcomes
- Discovery of governance models where accountability is shared or assigned to agents
- Case studies of humans becoming passive and companies failing as a result

---

## Where to Look for Answers

- HCI research on human-AI collaboration
- AI ethics and responsible AI frameworks (e.g., IEEE, EU AI Act)
- Case studies of RPA failures and agentic AI governance failures
- Founder essays on running AI-augmented companies

---

## Medha-Specific Translation

For Medha:
- Founder writes OKRs, runs customer calls, signs deals, makes pivots
- Agents draft, schedule, monitor, and surface exceptions
- Every external message or spend requires human approval
- Weekly scorecard review is the human's primary oversight ritual
