# Q5: What Is the Economics of an Agentic Enterprise?

**Research Question:** How does an agentic enterprise create, capture, and distribute value — and what does this mean for costs, labor, and business models?

---

## Why This Matters for Medha

If agents reduce labor costs but increase compute and complexity costs, the net effect matters. We need an economic model that shows whether agentic operations make Medha more sustainable.

---

## Sub-Questions

### 5.1 Cost Structure
1. What are the main cost categories of an agentic enterprise? (compute, APIs, orchestration, observability, human oversight)
2. How do these costs scale with agent usage?
3. What is the break-even point compared to human labor?
4. What hidden costs appear at scale? (debugging, governance, retries, hallucination cleanup)

### 5.2 Value Creation
1. Where do agents create the most value? (speed, scale, consistency, availability, decision quality)
2. How do we measure value created per agent?
3. What is the difference between cost savings and revenue enablement?
4. Which agentic capabilities are defensible vs. commoditized?

### 5.3 Labor and Employment
1. If agents replace tasks, what happens to the humans who did them?
2. Can an agentic enterprise be a force for better work (less drudgery) rather than job loss?
3. What new jobs does it create?
4. How do we think about "agent labor" ethically?

### 5.4 Business Model Implications
1. Does being agentic change what Medha can sell or how it prices?
2. Can agentic delivery enable new pricing models (outcome-based, usage-based, autonomous subscriptions)?
3. How do customers perceive value when agents deliver it?
4. What is the margin profile of an agentic vs. traditional SaaS?

### 5.5 Distribution and Ownership
1. Who owns the value created by agents? (founder, investors, customers, open-source community)
2. Are there models for shared ownership or cooperative agentic enterprises?
3. How do open-source contributors participate in value creation?
4. What is the long-term sustainability model?

---

## Current Hypotheses

1. The immediate economic benefit for Medha is **founder time reclaimed** — measured in hours saved per week.
2. Agent costs (API + compute + orchestration) should be <$100/month for the first phase.
3. The defensible value is not the agents themselves but the **domain-specific workflows and governance** Medha builds around them.
4. An agentic enterprise can be more humane if it uses agents to eliminate tedious work and lets humans focus on judgment and relationships.

---

## What Would Change Our Mind

- Evidence that agent costs exceed human labor costs at Medha's scale
- Discovery of a viable cooperative or shared-ownership model for agentic enterprises
- Case studies showing agentic businesses have structurally worse margins

---

## Where to Look for Answers

- Economic analysis of AI adoption in SMBs
- Case studies of solo-founder AI companies
- Research on future of work and AI labor
- Cooperative/DAO economic models
- Construction industry cost-benefit studies of automation

---

## Medha-Specific Translation

For Medha:
- Target: each agent saves at least 2x its cost in founder time
- Track cost per agent per week
- Price Medha's product based on value (rework avoided, time saved), not agent count
- Keep infrastructure costs low through self-hosting and model selection
