## 6. Role-Specific Interview Question Guides

### 6.1 VDC Coordinators

**Opening:** "Walk me through a typical Tuesday. Start from when you get in."

**Clash Detection:**
- "Tell me about the last time a clash made it to the field that shouldn't have. Start from the beginning."
- "How was it discovered? What tools were you using? How long did it take to resolve?"
- "Who else got pulled in? What did that delay cost?"

**Submittal Review:**
- "When was the last time you had to review submittals against specs? Walk me through that."
- "How do you know if the submittal matches the spec section?"
- "What do you do when you find a discrepancy? How do you track where you left off?"

**RFI Drafting:**
- "Tell me about the last RFI you had to write or expedite."
- "What triggered it — a drawing conflict, a spec gap, or a field condition?"
- "How long did it take from first noticing the issue to getting it submitted?"
- "What happened while you were waiting for the response?"

**Platform Pain:**
- "How many different software platforms do you touch in a typical day?"
- "Tell me about the last time you had to copy-paste information from one platform to another."
- "If you could wave a wand and change one thing about how coordination information flows, what would it be?"

### 6.2 Project Managers

**Opening:** "What does your typical week look like when you're juggling two or three active projects?"

**Document Search:**
- "Tell me about the last time you needed to find a specific document fast — like a contract clause or an approved submittal."
- "Where did you look first? How long did it take?"

**Dispute Prep:**
- "Have you ever had to prepare documentation for a dispute or claim? Tell me about that."
- "How did you gather the paper trail? What was missing that you wished you had?"

**RFI Tracking:**
- "How do you know if an RFI is blocking critical path work right now?"
- "When was the last time an unanswered RFI caused a schedule slip?"

**Severity Probe:**
- "How much time do you spend each week just looking for information you know exists somewhere?"

### 6.3 Document Controllers

**Opening:** "Walk me through what happens when a new drawing revision comes in from the architect."

**Version Control:**
- "Tell me about the last time someone on site built from an outdated drawing. How was that caught?"
- "How do you make sure everyone is working from the current revision?"

**Transmittals:**
- "How do you handle a transmittal that goes to 15 different subcontractors? Walk me through that."
- "How do you track who has acknowledged receipt? What happens when someone says they never got it?"

**Pain Quantification:**
- "How many documents do you process in a typical week? How many hours does that take?"

### 6.4 MEP Coordinators

**Opening:** "What's the most complex MEP coordination challenge you're dealing with right now?"

**Clash Escalation:**
- "Tell me about the last time you found a clash that required a design change rather than just a field adjustment."
- "How did you document it? Who did you have to convince? How long did it take to get a decision?"

**Spec Cross-Reference:**
- "When you're checking if an MEP submittal matches the spec, what's your process?"
- "How do you cross-reference between Div 23 and the drawings?"
- "What do you do when the spec and the drawing conflict?"

**Coordination Friction:**
- "Tell me about the last coordination meeting where a model issue caused real tension between trades."
- "What would have prevented it?"

## 7. The Mom Test Rules (Applied to Construction)

| Rule | Application |
|------|-------------|
| **Talk about their life, not your idea.** | Never pitch AI document intelligence. Ask about their Tuesday. |
| **Ask about specifics in the past, not opinions about the future.** | "Would you use this?" = poison. "Tell me about the last time..." = gold. |
| **Talk less, listen more.** | 70/30 rule. They talk, you probe. |
| **Map the buying committee.** | After every interview, ask: "Who else is involved in this decision? Who would block this? Who controls the budget?" |

### Questions That Kill (Don't Ask These)

| ❌ Bad Question | Why It Fails |
|---------------|-------------|
| "Would you use a platform that auto-detects contradictions?" | Produces polite lies. 83% who say "yes" never act. |
| "How frustrating is it to manage RFIs in email?" | Leading. Pushes them to agree. |
| "Don't you think clash detection takes too long?" | Leading. Pollutes data. |
| "What features would you want?" | Customers are bad at designing solutions. |

### Questions That Reveal Truth (Ask These)

| ✅ Good Question | What It Reveals |
|----------------|-----------------|
| "Walk me through how an RFI gets from the field to the architect." | Actual workflow, not idealized workflow. |
| "Tell me about the last time you found a clash after it was already in the field." | Real failure mode, severity, cost. |
| "How much time did that take you last week?" | Quantified pain for pricing later. |
| "What happened when that went wrong?" | Consequences and escalation path. |
| "What did you try before your current solution?" | Switching costs and competitive landscape. |

## 8. Compensation & Ethics

### Incentive Guidelines

| Participant Level | Recommended Incentive | Notes |
|-------------------|----------------------|-------|
| Individual Contributors (Document Controllers, Junior VDC) | $25–50 gift card | Appropriate and appreciated |
| Mid-Level (VDC Coordinators, MEP Coordinators, PMs) | $50–100 gift card OR value exchange | Offer choice: gift card OR research findings |
| Senior / Director+ (BIM Managers, VP Operations) | Value-first approach | Research report, executive summary, or charity donation. $50 can feel insulting. |

### Ethical Considerations

1. **Transparency:** Disclose recording, how data will be used, who will see it.
2. **Anonymization:** Commit to anonymizing quotes. Construction is a small world.
3. **No Coercion:** Incentive compensates time, not biases responses.
4. **IRB Compliance:** If formally affiliated with IIT Madras, check if IRB approval is required.

## 9. Interview Checklist

### Before
- [ ] Write down 3 hypotheses you want to test
- [ ] Define what evidence would prove you wrong
- [ ] Research the person's role and company
- [ ] Prepare 5–7 open-ended questions
- [ ] Test recording setup

### During
- [ ] Frame: "This is research, not sales. No right answers."
- [ ] Qualify: Confirm they handle the workflows you're studying
- [ ] Listen 70% of the time
- [ ] Ask "Tell me about the last time..." for every pain point
- [ ] Probe with "What happened next?" and "Why?"
- [ ] End with: "Who else should I talk to?" and "Can I follow up?"

### After
- [ ] Write notes within 2 hours
- [ ] Tag quotes with emotional intensity
- [ ] Track: Problem mentioned? Unprompted? Severity quantified? Current solution named?
- [ ] Send thank-you + incentive within 24 hours
