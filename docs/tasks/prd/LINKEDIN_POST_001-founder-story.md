# LinkedIn Post: Founder Story — Medha

**Option 1: The Researcher-Journey Post (Recommended)**

---

For the past few years, I've been obsessed with a single question:

**Why do construction projects still fail because of documents?**

Not because we lack tools. We have Revit, Navisworks, ACC, Procore, Bluebeam, Aconex. The problem is that none of them talk to each other. A VDC coordinator spends 2.5 hours a day just switching between platforms, cross-referencing, searching for the "right version" of a drawing.

I saw this up close during my time at IIT Madras, working with Sub-engineering teams and watching how document errors cascade into field rework. The Navigant study says RFIs cost $859K per project on average. I've watched that happen in real time.

So I started building **Medha**.

Not "another construction platform" — we've got enough of those. Medha is an intelligence layer that sits across the tools you already use. It reads your specs, drawings, RFIs, and emails. It remembers relationships between documents. And it warns you when two documents contradict each other *before* someone builds from the wrong version.

The underlying idea is called **MeMo** — Memory as a Model. Instead of searching documents every time you ask a question, we train a dedicated memory model on your project documents so the knowledge lives inside the model itself. Faster than RAG. More accurate under noise. And it actually understands cross-document relationships.

We just finished TEST-001: running the MeMo pipeline on real US Army Corps specs, university HVAC standards, and federal wage determinations. The pipeline works. It finds cross-document relationships. It catches contradictions.

There's still a long road ahead — especially for Dubai/GCC codes and real project addenda — but this is the first time I've felt like the core thesis is solid.

If you're a VDC coordinator, construction PM, or document controller who lives in Revit/Navisworks/ACC/Procore hell, I'd love to hear from you. What document problem costs you the most time? What's the one contradiction you caught too late?

**Option 2: The Problem-First Hook**

---

The most expensive mistakes in construction don't happen on site.

They happen in a PDF that nobody read carefully enough. In a spec section that contradicts a drawing. In an addendum that didn't get distributed to the right subcontractor. In an RFI that sat unanswered for three weeks.

I've been working on this problem for years, and today I'm sharing what we've built:

**Medha** — a context-preserving intelligence layer for construction documents.

It doesn't replace your platforms. It reads across them: Revit, Navisworks, ACC, Procore, Bluebeam, email. It extracts facts from specs and drawings. It consolidates redundant information. It verifies self-containment. And most importantly, it surfaces contradictions across documents.

The tech is based on **MeMo (Memory as a Model)** — arXiv:2605.15156 — adapted for construction. We use a frozen executive model + a trained memory model that internalizes cross-document relationships parametrically.

We just validated it on real construction documents: Kentucky Division 23 HVAC specs, UMD HVAC specs, and US Army Corps project specifications. The pipeline found real cross-document connections. It caught a deliberate contradiction we injected into real spec text.
