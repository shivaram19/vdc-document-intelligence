## 3b. Soft Problem-First Twitter Thread: "We See It Too"

*Tone: Empathetic, consultative, no blame. The problem is presented as something the industry shares, not a failure of the buyer.*

1/ Every construction project starts with the same hope: this time, the documents will line up.

Specs. Drawings. Addenda. RFIs. Submittals.

They should tell one story. Often, they don't.

🧵

2/ It's not because anyone is careless.

It's because the work is enormous. Thousands of pages. Dozens of revisions. Multiple disciplines. Tight deadlines.

The human brain wasn't built to hold all of that in working memory.

3/ And yet, teams are asked to do exactly that.

Cross-check specs against drawings. Catch the addendum that changed the rating but not the detail. Notice the RFI answer that quietly conflicts with the bulletin.

Most of the time, people get it right. That's the miracle.

4/ But "most of the time" is expensive in construction.

One missed contradiction becomes a submittal rejection. Then an RFI. Then a change order. Then rework in the field.

By then, the cost isn't measured in hours. It's measured in concrete, steel, and schedule.

5/ We started Medha because we kept hearing the same thing from VDC teams:

"We know we're missing things. We just don't know which things until it's too late."

6/ That stuck with us.

Because the problem isn't that teams aren't trying. The problem is that the system asks them to be perfect at a scale where perfection isn't reasonable.

7/ So we're building something that acts like a second pair of eyes.

It reads the documents. It remembers every version. It flags where specs, drawings, addenda, and RFIs stop agreeing.

Then it gets out of the way and lets the experts decide.

8/ Not a replacement. Not an autopilot. Just a way to reduce the noise before it becomes a problem.

9/ The goal is simple: fewer surprises. Less time hunting. More confidence that what you're about to build matches what the documents say.

10/ If your team spends too much time cross-checking documents and still worries about what slipped through, you're not alone.
