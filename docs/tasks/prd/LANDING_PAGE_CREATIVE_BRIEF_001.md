# Medha Landing Page — Creative Brief

**Project:** Medha buyer-centered landing page (medha.trayini.ai)  
**Date:** 2026-06-12  
**Goal:** Convert VDC/BIM/GC visitors into pilot conversations by making them feel that Medha solves a problem they already live with every day.

---

## 1. Core Insight

Construction projects do not fail because teams are careless. They fail because the documents disagree with each other and no one catches it in time.

Medha's landing page must make the visitor feel:

> *"This tool understands exactly why my last project bled money — and it would have caught it."*

---

## 2. Audience

### Primary
- **VDC / BIM Coordinators** — spend hours cross-referencing specs and drawings
- **GC Project Engineers** — bridge field questions back to the documents
- **Preconstruction Directors** — own risk before ground breaks
- **VDC Agency Owners** — need a real product they can stand behind

### What they all have in common
They have already opened 50 PDFs today. They do not want another "AI dashboard." They want fewer surprises, faster answers, and a cleaner submittal log.

---

## 3. Positioning Statement

**For** VDC teams and general contractors who review construction documents,  
**Medha** is a document contradiction detector  
**that** reads drawings, specs, RFIs, and addenda as one connected set  
**unlike** generic AI search tools,  
**because** it flags mismatches with cited sources before they become rework or change orders.

---

## 4. The One Sentence

> Medha catches the contradictions hiding in your construction documents before they cost you.

---

## 5. Emotional Arc

The page must move the visitor through six feelings in order:

| Section | Feeling | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | Recognition | They must immediately see their own problem reflected. |
| Cost of the Problem | Urgency | They need to feel the financial and schedule pain. |
| How It Works | Relief | The solution must look simpler than their current process. |
| Pilot Offer | Low Risk | They must believe they can try it without committing. |
| Trust | Credibility | They need proof this is built for construction, not Silicon Valley hype. |
| Close | Momentum | One clear action, no hesitation. |

## 6. Page Structure

### Section 1 — Hero: "The contradiction is already in the documents."

**Headline options:**
- *"The contradiction is already in the documents. Most teams find it too late."*
- *"Your drawings and specs disagree. Find out before the field does."*
- *"Before the first wall goes up, make sure the documents agree."*

**Subhead:**
> Medha reads your drawings, specs, RFIs, and addenda as one connected set, and flags the mismatches that slip past manual review.

**CTA:**
- Primary: *"Reserve your free pilot"*
- Secondary: *"See how it works"*

**Interaction note:**  
Use a split-screen or layered reveal. One side shows a drawing note. The other side shows a spec paragraph. They visibly contradict. A subtle pulse or highlight draws the eye to the mismatch. This is the "crazy" visual hook — but the content is a real construction contradiction.

---

### Section 2 — The Cost: "One missed contradiction can cost more than a year of software."

**Headline:**
> One missed mismatch can cost more than a year of software.

**Body:**
> Document errors are a leading driver of construction rework. RFIs multiply when answers are buried across disconnected sheets. Change orders often trace back to contradictions that could have been caught before ground broke.

**Visual:**  
Three cards or panels:
1. **Rework** — "Document-related errors are a leading source of cost overrun."
2. **RFI avalanche** — "When answers are buried, teams generate more RFIs than necessary."
3. **Late surprises** — "The cheapest place to catch a mismatch is before the pour."

**Interaction note:**  
Scroll-triggered. Each card locks into view as the user scrolls. Use a subtle counter or cost estimator if you have real numbers. If not, keep it qualitative and honest.

---

### Section 3 — The Mechanic: "A second pair of eyes that has already read everything."

**Headline:**
> A second pair of eyes that has already read everything.

**Body:**
> Medha does not replace your BIM coordinator. It is the intern who has already opened every PDF, checked every addendum, and remembered where the concrete strength is hidden.

**Four-step walkthrough:**
1. **Upload your project documents** — PDFs, Word files, text, scanned sheets.
2. **Ask in plain language** — "What concrete strength do the column notes require?"
3. **Get contradiction flags** — When two documents disagree, Medha surfaces both sides.
4. **Export findings** — Pull flags into RFIs, submittals, or coordination meetings.

**Interaction note:**  
Make this a horizontal scroll or step-by-step animation. Each step reveals the next with a subtle transition. Step 3 is the climax: two documents side by side, a contradiction banner slides in.

---

### Section 4 — The Pilot: "Try it on your documents this week."

**Headline:**
> Try Medha on your documents this week.

**Body:**
> We are accepting a small number of free pilots ahead of Sunday's production release. Send us one active or recent project. We will tune Medha to your document structure and show you the contradictions it finds.

**Three promises:**
- No generic demo. Real documents.
- No upfront commitment.
- No obligation if it does not save time in the first week.

**Interaction note:**  
This section should feel like a form but read like a conversation. A single question at a time: role, company, project, email. Progress bar optional.

---

### Section 5 — Trust: "Built for construction, not for hype."

**Headline:**
> Built for construction, not for hype.

**Trust points:**
- **Cited answers** — every response points back to the source page.
- **Confidence thresholds** — if the answer is not in the documents, Medha says so.
- **Per-project isolation** — each job gets its own index. No data bleed.
- **Local or cloud** — run offline or use cloud models.

**Interaction note:**  
Use a blueprint/grid aesthetic. Avoid orbs, gradients, and AI clichés. Think engineering drawing, not SaaS dashboard.

## 7. Visual Direction

### Mood
- **Engineering blueprint**, not cyberpunk AI.
- **Confident and calm**, not frenetic.
- **Construction site meets design studio.**

### Color palette
- Deep blueprint navy: `#0f1b2e`
- Warm warning amber for contradiction flags: `#c06c28`
- Clean off-white background: `#fafbfc`
- Safety green for trust signals: `#2f6f45`

### Typography
- System font stack or a clean sans-serif.
- No overused "AI-default" display fonts.
- Monospace for technical identifiers and citations.

### Imagery
- Avoid stock photos of hard hats and laptops.
- Use abstracted document layouts, blueprint grids, and annotation marks.
- The hero visual should feel like a magnifying glass moving over a drawing set.

---

## 8. Interaction Principles

1. **Every animation must earn its keep.** If it does not clarify the contradiction problem, cut it.
2. **Scroll is the primary interaction.** Do not hide content behind clicks.
3. **The contradiction reveal is the signature moment.** Build the page toward it.
4. **Mobile is non-negotiable.** Most LinkedIn traffic will be on phones.
5. **Load fast.** No heavy JS frameworks. Self-contained HTML/CSS/JS.

---

## 9. Tone and Language Rules

### Use
- Concrete construction language: drawings, specs, RFIs, addenda, submittals, change orders.
- Active voice.
- Short paragraphs.
- "You" and "your project."

### Avoid (AI slop)
- "AI-powered"
- "Unlock insights"
- "Leverage"
- "Transform"
- "Synergy"
- "Intelligent platform"
- "Revolutionize"
- Generic buzzwords without a specific construction meaning.

---

## 10. Conversion Strategy

### Primary conversion
Reserve a free pilot.

### Secondary conversion
Email or LinkedIn direct reach-out.

### Urgency
- Production release this Sunday.
- Limited pilot spots.
- Real person replies within one business day.

### Objection handling
Include a short FAQ:
- "Will this replace my BIM coordinators?" → No. It augments them.
- "What documents can we upload?" → PDFs, Word, text, scanned sheets.
- "How long before we see value?" → Same hour upload; useful flags in the first project.
- "Do we need to train a model?" → No. It uses retrieval over your existing documents.
