The problem

The documents should tell one story.

Every construction project starts with the same hope: this time, the documents will line up. Then the drawings, specs, addenda, and RFIs arrive from different teams, in different versions, and the contradictions start to hide.

Drawing vs. spec mismatch

The structural spec calls for 5,000 psi concrete. The drawing notes show 4,000 psi. Which one governs?

Buried answers

A single question can mean opening fifteen PDFs, checking three addenda, and still not being sure you found the latest revision.

RFI avalanche

When documents disagree, coordination turns into a stream of RFIs that eat time from engineers, coordinators, and field staff.

Late surprises

The cheapest place to catch a contradiction is before ground breaks. The most expensive place is in the field.

The cost

One missed contradiction can cost more than a year of software.

Research ties document errors directly to rework, delays, and claims. The cost rarely shows up as a line item until it is already in the field.

Rework is expensive Document-related errors are a leading driver of construction rework and cost overrun.
RFIs multiply When answers are buried across disconnected documents, teams generate more RFIs than necessary.
Change orders trace back Many change orders originate in contradictions that could have been caught during preconstruction.

How it works

Four steps. No new workflow to learn.

Upload your project documents

PDFs, Word files, text, and scanned sheets. One project, one connected index. No manual tagging required.

Ask in plain language

"What concrete strength do the column notes require?" Get an answer with the source page attached, not a guess.

Get contradiction flags

When two documents disagree, Medha surfaces both sides so you can decide before it becomes an RFI.

Export findings

Pull flags into your existing workflow: RFIs, submittals, coordination meetings, or reports.
