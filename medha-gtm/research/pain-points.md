## Job 1: Instant Spec Lookup
> *"Find the spec clause I need before the concrete truck arrives"*

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Functional** | Retrieve document facts in <10 seconds |
| **Emotional** | Feel confident, not frantic |
| **Social** | Look prepared to the superintendent |
| **Current workaround** | Phone the office, Ctrl+F 50 PDFs, ask senior engineer |
| **Cost of failure** | Pour delay ($5K–$50K), concrete waste, schedule slip |

**Why questions to ask:**
- "Why does it take 20 minutes to find a spec clause that IS in the documents?"
- "Why do field engineers call the office instead of reading the spec themselves?"
- "Why don't your digital document tools solve this already?"

---

## Job 2: Contradiction Detection
> *"Catch contradictions before they cost me $50K in rework"*

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Functional** | Scan specs vs. drawings automatically |
| **Emotional** | Sleep better, reduce anxiety |
| **Social** | Protect reputation as careful PM |
| **Current workaround** | Manual cross-check, hope the sub notices, learn at pour |
| **Cost of failure** | Rework, claims, litigation, reputation damage |

**Evidence:**
- Zipboard 2023: "Discrepancies regarding specifications or conflicts between drawings and specifications"
- Liverpool Univ BIM Risk Study: 17 legal risks from BIM document conflicts
- Palo Alto City Auditor: "Poor document management prevents defending claims"

**Why questions:**
- "Why do contradictions still make it to site on digital projects?"
- "Why does clash detection find model conflicts but not spec-drawing conflicts?"
- "Why did your last rework event happen? Was it discoverable in the documents?"

## Job 3: RFI Drafting
> *"Draft RFIs that architects actually answer quickly"*

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Functional** | Auto-reference source documents |
| **Emotional** | Feel competent, not annoying |
| **Social** | Be seen as professional, not clueless |
| **Current workaround** | Write from memory, no citations, vague questions |
| **Cost of failure** | Delayed response, back-and-forth, architect frustration |

**Evidence:**
- Hughes et al. (2013): Average 887 RFIs per project in Americas
- Industry benchmark: 10 RFIs per $1M contract value
- Standard RFI specs: "Frivolous RFIs" = info already in Contract Documents

**Why questions:**
- "Why do architects take 2 weeks to answer some RFIs?"
- "Why are 30–40% of RFIs deemed 'frivolous'?"
- "Why doesn't your RFI system auto-cite the spec section?"

## Job 5: Audit & Compliance
> *"Prove to the owner we checked everything"*

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Functional** | Generate audit trails + evidence |
| **Emotional** | Feel defensible in disputes |
| **Social** | Demonstrate professionalism for future bids |
| **Current workaround** | Sign-off sheets, manual checklists, hope |
| **Cost of failure** | Dispute loss, non-compliance, lost bids |

**Evidence:**
- Palo Alto City Auditor: "Unable to support and defend key decisions"
- UK BIM Level 3: Mandated audit trails
- Singapore CORENET X: Digital submission verification

**Why questions:**
- "Why can't you prove compliance without manual document review?"
- "Why does every project handover lose critical documentation?"
- "Why are disputes resolved by 'who has the better lawyer' instead of facts?"
