# Response Decoder
## What They Say vs. What They Mean

| They Say | It Means | Your Response |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| "That's interesting." | Nothing. Zero signal. | "What specifically interests you?" |
| "I would buy it IF it had X." | Not buying. Feature = deflection. | "Would you buy it without X?" |
| "Let me think about it." | Soft no. | "What specifically do you need to think through?" |
| "I need to ask my boss." | Possible. Or polite exit. | "What would your boss need to see?" |
| "Can you send me a deck?" | Trying to end call. | "Happy to. What's the decision process?" |
| "This would save us so much time." | Still not commitment. | "Would you be willing to run a pilot?" |
| **"When can we start?"** | **Buy signal. Close now.** | **"I can have you set up by Tuesday."** |
| **"I want this for [other project]."** | **Champion behavior.** | **"Who should I talk to? Can you intro me?"** |

## The 5 Whys for Customer Discovery

When a prospect mentions a pain:
1. "Why is that a problem?"
2. "Why does that keep happening?"
3. "Why haven't you fixed it yet?"
4. "Why would solving that matter to your boss?"
5. "Why is NOW the right time to address it?"

## The Switch Interview

- "What made you start looking for a solution?"
- "What pushed you from 'this is annoying' to 'I need to fix this'?"
- "What did you try before? Why didn't it work?"
- "What would make you switch from your current approach?"
