### 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Construction is the world’s largest industry (~13 % of global GDP, ≈ $13 trillion in output) yet has lagged in productivity (≈ 1 % annual growth for two decades) and digital maturity.  Leading business schools are converging on four levers of transformation: **(i) platform business models & data monetization, (ii) a radical shift from project‑based to product‑based delivery, (iii) scaled adoption of AI, robotics and digital twins, and (iv) sustainability‑driven industrialization.**  

Market forecasts point to a construction‑technology market of **$5.7 B–$7.0 B in 2025** growing at **12–17 % CAGR**, and a broader **building‑technology market of $152 B in 2025 heading to $325 B by 2036**.  McKinsey estimates that **$265 B in annual profit pools** are up for grabs for players that move fast, while **40–45 % of incumbent value‑added is at risk**.  

For Trayini.ai, the strategic imperative is clear: treat data as a core asset, move toward repeatable productized offerings, and position as a platform orchestrator rather than a traditional project‑based contractor.

### 11. STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR TRELO LABS

| Imperative | Rationale & Action |
|---|---|
| **Build the Data Layer** | Follow MIT and Stanford frameworks by treating project data as a strategic asset.  Monetize via benchmarking, predictive analytics, and digital‑twin services ( Data‑as‑a‑Service can deliver ~ 8 % revenue streams). |
