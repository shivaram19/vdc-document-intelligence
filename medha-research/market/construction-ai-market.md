## Growth Drivers
1. Labor shortages in construction
2. Project delays and cost overruns
3. Increasing demand for data integration and visual analytics
4. Integration of AI with IoT and BIM
5. Rise of generative AI in design and scheduling
6. SaaS-based AI solutions adoption
7. Government initiatives for smart cities

## Key Barriers
1. Lack of skilled workforce for AI development
2. Messy document inputs (scans, mixed templates)
3. Privacy and contractual constraints on real project data
4. Integration frictions with entrenched tools
5. Cost and expertise required to fine-tune AI models
6. Resistance to change in traditional construction workflows
7. Interoperability issues between software platforms

## Document-Centric AI Gap
"Most of these workflows still rely on manual copying, cross-checking, and routing among stakeholders, making them labor-intensive and error-prone... This remains one of the least explored frontiers for AI, despite its centrality to project administration."
- Frontiers in Built Environment, 2025

## AI Adoption Timeline
- **2024:** Inflection point - predictive AI mature, generative AI entering production, agentic AI beginning pilots
- **2025:** Industrial copilots launched, construction simulation software mainstream
- **2026:** 18% industry size growth projected
- **2029-2035:** Full market maturation
