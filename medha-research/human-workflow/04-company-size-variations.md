# How Document Review Varies by Company Size and Type

## Large General Contractors (Turner, Clark, Whiting-Turner, Kiewit, Mortenson)

### Scale
- Turner employs **1,000+ VDC engineers** nationwide integrated into every office
- Dedicated BIM coordinators, VDC managers, modelers, and prefabrication specialists
- Centralized VDC departments provide services to multiple business units

### Technology Depth
- Full BIM/VDC stack: Revit, Navisworks, BIM 360, ACC, laser scanning, 3D printing, 4D/5D modeling, model-based layout with robotic total stations

### Process Maturity
- Formal BIM Execution Plans (BEP)
- Standardized clash resolution workflows
- Regular coordination meetings with formal issue tracking
- VDC integral from preconstruction through closeout

### Document Review Approach
- **Specialized teams**: One person may focus only on MEP coordination, another only on structural
- **Heavy process**: Multi-stage review gates, formal sign-offs, extensive documentation
- **Early involvement**: VDC participates in design development, not just construction documents
- **Model-centric**: 3D clash detection is the primary review method; 2D is secondary

---

## Mid-Size Contractors ($20M-$200M annual volume)

### Scale
- Hybrid roles: One person may serve as both BIM Coordinator and Project Engineer
- Selective BIM use: Often use BIM only on complex projects or where owner-mandated

### Technology
- Revit and Navisworks for coordination
- Less use of 4D/5D or advanced visualization
- May rely more on 2D overlay review and field coordination than full 3D clash detection

### Document Review Approach
- **Pragmatic**: Focus on high-impact clashes and coordination issues
- **Budget-constrained**: Less budget for dedicated VDC staff; coordinators often juggle multiple projects
- **Reactive**: More fire-fighting than proactive coordination
- **Mixed 2D/3D**: Bluebeam overlays still heavily used alongside Navisworks

---

## Specialized VDC Agencies / Third-Party MEP Coordinators

### Scale
- Union-trade MEP coordinators bring 5-year apprenticeship field experience PLUS digital expertise
- Understand what is actually constructible, not just what the model shows

### Focus
- **Trade depth**: Deep knowledge of specific trades (MEP, structural, architectural)
- **Dedicated focus**: Unlike GC-led coordination (which competes with schedule/budget/safety priorities), third-party coordinators focus exclusively on clash-free, code-compliant models
- **Code expertise**: Deep knowledge of trade-specific regulations (NFPA, NEC, ASHRAE)
- **Prefabrication support**: Produce LOD 400 models for fabrication and robotic layout

### Document Review Approach
- **Extremely thorough**: Their reputation depends on catching everything
- **Field-aware**: They know which clashes matter for installation and which don't
- **Code-first**: They verify code compliance as part of coordination, not as a separate step
- **Neutral**: They serve multiple stakeholders simultaneously (owner, architect, GC, subs)

---

## Key Differences Summary

| Aspect | Large GCs | Mid-Size Contractors | VDC Agencies |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Team size** | Dedicated VDC dept | Hybrid roles | Specialized consultants |
| **Review depth** | Deep, process-driven | Pragmatic, risk-focused | Extremely thorough |
| **Technology** | Full stack | Core tools only | Best-in-class for specialty |
| **Timing** | Early involvement | Construction docs phase | On-demand, project-specific |
| **Primary method** | 3D clash detection | Mixed 2D/3D | Model + field verification |
| **Deliverables** | 4D/5D models, prefab packages | Coordination reports | LOD 400, shop drawings, RFI logs |
| **Cost model** | Internal overhead | Project-specific budget | Fee-for-service |
