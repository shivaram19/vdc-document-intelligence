# BIM/VDC Coordinator: Hour-by-Hour Daily Responsibilities

## The Reality of the Role

BIM constitutes only about **7% of total project hours**. On a 6-month infrastructure project valued at 2,000 man-hours, a BIM Coordinator may only have ~140 BIM hours (about 25 hours/month). This forces most coordinators to work on **4+ projects simultaneously**, creating a highly fragmented, reactive schedule.

## Typical Daily Schedule

### 7:00–8:00 AM — Arrival & Model Triage
- Check emails, Slack, and project management platforms for overnight model updates, clash reports, or field issues
- Review clash detection reports from Navisworks/Revit (often run overnight)
- Confirm which drawing revision is current before starting any work

### 8:00–9:00 AM — Coordination Meetings
- Daily standup with project engineers, superintendents, or trade foremen
- Review previous day's model changes and assign clash resolutions to specific trades
- Discuss fabrication deadlines and whether coordinated models are ready for shop drawing generation

### 9:00–11:00 AM — Clash Resolution & Model Management
- Work directly in Navisworks or Revit to resolve hard/soft clashes
- Validate subcontractor models before accepting them into the federated model
- Ensure models comply with the BIM Execution Plan (BEP) and specified LOD

### 11:00 AM–12:00 PM — Cross-Discipline Review
- Overlay structural, architectural, and MEP models to verify spatial coordination
- Check for constructability issues (ductwork through structural beams, insufficient ceiling cavity space)
- Issue RFIs when design intent is unclear

### 12:00–1:00 PM — Lunch (often working)
- Frequently interrupted by field calls about model discrepancies or layout issues

### 1:00–3:00 PM — Technical Support & Training
- Respond to ad-hoc requests from project team members on Revit/Navisworks workflows
- Train junior staff or trade contractors on model access, viewer tools, or markup procedures

### 3:00–5:00 PM — Reporting & Documentation
- Generate coordination reports, clash matrices, and model-based quantity takeoffs
- Update issue tracking logs (BIM 360, Procore, or ACC)
- Prepare 3D visualizations, 4D sequencing models, or prefabrication drawings

### 5:00–6:00 PM — Client/Stakeholder Updates
- Present model status to owners, architects, or VDC managers
- Distribute summary reports on model health, open clashes, and estimated resolution dates

### After Hours (Common)
- Many BIM coordinators report checking emails/Slack in the evening due to multiple project deadlines

## Core Responsibilities
- Federated model compilation and management
- Clash detection and resolution assignment
- Model validation against BEP requirements
- Coordination meeting facilitation
- Shop drawing validation
- 4D/5D model support (schedule and cost integration)
- Training and technical support
- Field verification using laser scans or model-based layout
