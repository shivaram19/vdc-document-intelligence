# Trade-Specific Document Review Checklists

## HVAC (Mechanical) — Division 23

### What Coordinators Check
- Duct sizes and CFM values against load calculations
- Static pressure and friction loss rates
- Supply/return duct sizing balance (returns should be equal to or larger than supply)
- VAV box locations and control sequences
- Coil capacities and entering/leaving air temperatures
- Refrigerant pipe sizes and insulation requirements
- Fan capacities, motor HP, and VFD requirements
- Fire/smoke damper locations at rated partitions
- Access doors for dampers, coils, and controls
- Seismic bracing requirements
- Coordination with architectural ceiling plans (diffuser alignment)

### Common Submittal Review Focus
- Refrigerant type, electrical data (voltage, phase, MCA, MOP)
- Coil coatings, warranty terms, spare parts requirements
- Multiple equipment tags properly broken out and reviewed individually
- AHU or chiller submittal can carry 60+ technical characteristics

## Structural — Divisions 03, 05, 31

### What Coordinators Check
- **Concrete**: Compressive strength (psi), water-cement ratio, slump, air entrainment, corrosion inhibitors
- **Rebar**: Grade (40, 60, A706), lap splice lengths, development lengths, hook details, clear cover
- **Steel**: Member sizes, connection types (bolted vs. welded), bolt grades (A325, A490), weld symbols and sizes
- **Load paths**: Continuous vertical and lateral load paths, drag struts, collectors, chord forces
- **Diaphragms**: Roof/floor deck attachment, edge angles, shear transfer details
- **Foundations**: Pier/pile capacities, embedment depths, bearing elevations
- **Deflection limits**: L/360 for floors, L/240 for roofs (typical)

### Common Errors Caught
- Missing structural support under heavy equipment
- Rebar shown too congested for concrete placement
- Reinforcing steel congestion in pier caps and hinges
- Vibrator space around reinforcing steel

## Electrical — Division 26

### What Coordinators Check
- **Panel schedules**: Circuit numbers, breaker sizes, wire sizes, load calculations
- **Voltage drop**: Typically limited to 3% for feeders, 5% overall
- **Conduit sizes**: Fill ratios per NEC (40% for 3+ conductors)
- **AIC ratings**: Available fault current vs. panel short-circuit current rating
- **Grounding and bonding**: Ground bus connections, grounding electrode conductors
- **Arc flash**: Coordination studies and labeling requirements
- **Lighting**: Fixture types, lens selections, emergency egress lighting, exit signs
- **Fire alarm**: Device placement per NFPA 72, smoke detector spacing

### Common Submittal Failures
- Voltage and phase mismatches
- SCCR values that don't match available fault current
- FLA/MCA/MOP data that conflicts with the panel schedule
- Lighting fixture schedules (lens type, finish, energy ratings)

## Plumbing — Divisions 22

### What Coordinators Check
- Fixture counts per code (IPC/UPC)
- Water supply pipe sizes and pressure requirements
- Drainage pipe slopes and venting configurations
- Grease waste interceptors (kitchen facilities)
- Backflow prevention devices
- Domestic hot water recirculation and balancing valves
- Roof drain sizing and secondary drainage
- Coordination with structural for pipe supports and seismic bracing

---

## Fire Protection — Division 21

### What Coordinators Check
- **Sprinkler coverage**: Maximum coverage area per head based on hazard classification (NFPA 13)
- **Hydraulic calculations**: Available water flow vs. system demand
- **Standpipes**: Location in stairwells, clearances, shut-off valves
- **Fire Department Connection (FDC)**: Height (18–36" above grade), proximity to test header
- **Fire pump room**: 2-hour rated enclosure, direct exterior access, maintenance clearances
- **Alarm devices**: Coordinated with electrical designer per NFPA 72
- **Sprinkler obstructions**: Heads located below ducts, beams, and overhead doors

### NFPA 13 Design Criteria
- Density of water to be applied (gpm/sq.ft.)
- Surface area covered by ceiling-mounted protection
- Sprinkler installation in racks for commodity storage
- Temperature ratings (intermediate, ordinary, high)

## Architectural — Divisions 08, 09, 10

### What Coordinators Check
- **Doors/Windows/Glazing**: Fire ratings and UL listing numbers, light transmittance/reflectance values, approved manufacturer verification
- **Finishes**: Floor-to-ceiling heights, R-values, window U-factors, interior finish schedules
- **ADA compliance**: Door widths, ramp slopes, restroom configurations, turning radius
- **Occupancy types**: Building area, floor heights, egress paths
- **Ceiling plans**: Diffuser alignment, light fixture placement, sprinkler head coordination

### Common Submittal Review Focus
- Approved manufacturer verification is critical
- Fire ratings and listing numbers require careful matching against the spec
- Shop drawings often include physical examples of finish and glazing specifications
