# Medha Customer Meeting Playbook
## How to Meet Prospects, Run Demos, and Get Real Buy Signals

**Purpose:** Turn construction professionals into design partners and paying customers  
**Method:** Problem-first discovery → Live demo → Commitment ask  
**Duration:** 30–45 minutes per meeting  
**Research Basis:** First Round PMF Method (2025), The Mom Test, Dorian Barker Founder-Led Sales (2025), Motionify Demo Science (2025)

---

## THE GOLDEN RULE

> **Never ask "Would you buy this?"**  
> **Never ask "Do you think this is a good idea?"**  
> **Never demo before you understand their pain.**

People lie to be nice. 35% of startups die because founders listened to polite lies (CB Insights).  
The only validation that matters: **commitment** (time, money, introductions, data).

## MEETING STRUCTURE: The 4-Phase Flow

```
Phase 1: Context (5 min)     → Build rapport, confirm role
Phase 2: Discovery (10 min)  → Uncover pain, NEVER pitch
Phase 3: Demo (15 min)       → Show solution to THEIR pain
Phase 4: Commitment (5 min)  → Ask for something real
```

---

## PHASE 1: CONTEXT (5 Minutes)

**Goal:** Make them comfortable. Confirm they have the authority + problem.

**Script:**
> "Thanks for making time. Before we jump in — can you tell me a bit about your role and what projects you're working on right now?"

**Listen for:**
- Project types (commercial, infra, residential)
- Team size
- Their specific title (VDC Manager = different pain than Project Director)
- Stress signals ("We're juggling 8 projects" = pain)

**Red flag:** If they're not involved in document coordination, RFI management, or QA — they can't validate your product. Politely end early: *"Sounds like this might not be the right fit for your role — is there someone on your team who handles document review or RFIs?"*

---

## PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (10 Minutes)

**Goal:** Uncover the Job-to-be-Done. Let THEM describe the pain.  
**Rule:** No pitching. No features. No "we built." Just questions.

### The 8 Discovery Questions (Pick 4–5 Based on Role)

#### For VDC Managers / BIM Leads
1. **"Walk me through the last time your team found a contradiction between a spec and a drawing. What happened?"**
2. **"How do your field engineers get answers when they're on site and don't have the spec book?"**
3. **"When a new project starts, how long does it take for someone new to get up to speed on the document set?"**
4. **"What does your document audit process look like before handover to the owner?"**

#### For Project Directors / Construction Managers
5. **"How many hours per week do your PMs spend searching for information across project documents?"**
6. **"Tell me about the most expensive rework event on your last project. Was it a document issue?"**
7. **"When you get an RFI from the contractor, how do you verify the answer is consistent with the original spec?"**
8. **"If I gave you a magic wand for one construction workflow, what would you fix?"**

#### For BIM Service Providers / Outsourcing Firms
9. **"How do you differentiate yourself from the 20 other firms offering BIM modeling?"**
10. **"When clients ask 'did you check everything?' — how do you prove it?"**
11. **"What's the biggest reason you lose a pitch to a competitor?"**

### Follow-Up Probes (The "Why" Drill)

When they mention a pain, drill deeper with:
- **"Why is that a problem?"**
- **"How often does that happen?"**
- **"What does that cost you?"** (time, money, reputation)
- **"What have you tried to fix it?"**
- **"Why didn't that work?"**
- **"Who else is affected when that happens?"**

**Document their exact words.** These become your marketing copy.

### Discovery Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Don't Ask | ✅ Ask Instead | Why |
|-------------|---------------|-----|
| "Would you use a tool that...?" | "How do you handle X today?" | Leading questions get false yeses |
| "Is document search a problem for you?" | "Walk me through your last document search." | Specific stories > generic complaints |
| "Do you think AI could help?" | "What takes up most of your non-billable time?" | Let them name the pain |
| "How much would you pay?" | "What did your last document management tool cost?" | Anchoring bias kills pricing discovery |

## PHASE 3: DEMO (15 Minutes)

**Goal:** Show Medha solving THEIR exact pain. Not a feature tour.

### The 5P Demo Framework (Motionify 2025)

```
Hook    (0–30s)   → Name THEIR pain
Promise (30–60s)  → "I'm going to show you how to solve that in 30 seconds"
Proof   (60–120s) → Live product, their project type, real documents
Path    (2–3min)  → "And here's how it fits into your workflow"
Push    (3–4min)  → Clear next step
```

### Demo Script: Medha VDC Document Intelligence

#### HOOK (0–30 seconds)
> "You mentioned that [SPECIFIC PAIN FROM DISCOVERY] — like when [SPECIFIC STORY THEY TOLD]. I'm going to show you how Medha handles exactly that."

#### PROMISE (30–60 seconds)
> "In the next few minutes, I'll show you three things:
> 1. How to find any spec clause in under 10 seconds
> 2. How to catch contradictions before they become RFIs
> 3. How to draft an RFI that references source documents automatically
>
> And this is all on your actual project documents — not a canned demo."

#### PROOF (60 seconds — Live Demo)

**Setup:** Have Medha open in a browser tab. Pre-load a project similar to theirs.

**Demo 1: Instant Spec Lookup (45 seconds)**
> "You said your PMs spend 20 minutes searching for clauses. Watch this."
> 
> [Type their actual question — e.g., "concrete compressive strength" or "standpipe spacing"]
> 
> "Here's the answer, with the exact source document cited. No Ctrl+F across 50 PDFs."

**Demo 2: Contradiction Scan (45 seconds)**
> "You told me about the time the drawing said 6 inches and the spec said 8. Let me show you how Medha catches that."
> 
> [Run scan_contradictions on the loaded project]
> 
> "Here — it flagged that the structural spec calls for 5,000 psi but the drawing notes say 4,000. This would have been caught before concrete pour."

**Demo 3: RFI Draft (30 seconds)**
> "And when you DO need to write an RFI — Medha drafts it with source citations."
> 
> [Type: "What is the concrete strength for column C-12?"]
> 
> "Here's a draft RFI, already referencing the spec section and drawing sheet. The architect gets a clear, sourced question — so they answer faster."

#### PATH (60 seconds — Integration + Security)
> "Now — this isn't replacing your BIM model or your project management tool. Medha sits alongside them. It reads your document set — PDFs, Word docs, even scanned drawings — and makes them queryable.
> 
> And because we're dealing with sensitive project data, every user has to prove they know the project before they get access. Three-factor authentication. Bank-grade security."

#### PUSH (CTA — 30 seconds)
> "What I just showed you — would that save time on [THEIR PROJECT NAME]?"

Wait for their answer. If yes → move to Phase 4.

## PHASE 4: COMMITMENT (5 Minutes)

**Goal:** Get a real commitment. Not a "sounds interesting." Something that costs them something.

### The Commitment Ladder (Least to Most)

| Level | Commitment | What It Costs Them | Signal Strength |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Give feedback** on your roadmap | 15 min of thought | Weak |
| 2 | **Introduce you** to a colleague | Social capital | Medium |
| 3 | **Share project documents** for a pilot | Trust + time | Strong |
| 4 | **Run a 30-day pilot** on active project | Time + reputation | Very strong |
| 5 | **Sign a letter of intent** | Legal credibility | Extremely strong |
| 6 | **Pre-pay** for annual license | Money | Validation |

### How to Ask (Exact Scripts)

**Level 3 — Share Documents (Strong Signal):**
> "This works best when trained on your actual documents. Would you be open to sharing a sample set — say, 5–10 specs and drawings from a completed project — so I can show you exactly what Medha would find?"

**Level 4 — 30-Day Pilot (Very Strong):**
> "What if we ran a pilot? You give me access to one active project's documents. I'll set up Medha. We measure: time saved on spec lookups, contradictions caught, RFIs drafted. If it doesn't save you 5 hours in the first month, we walk away. Does that feel fair?"

**Level 6 — Pre-Pay (Validation):**
> "I'm offering the first 10 design partners a 50% discount on the first year, locked in forever. The catch: I need your feedback weekly for the first month. Would you be willing to pre-pay ₹X to lock that in?"

### The "Mom Test" Response Decoder

| They Say | It Means | Your Response |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| "That's interesting." | Nothing. Zero signal. | "What specifically interests you?" |
| "I would buy it IF it had X." | They're not buying. Feature requests = deflection. | "Would you buy it without X? If not, X isn't the real problem." |
| "Let me think about it." | Soft no. | "What specifically do you need to think through?" |
| "I need to ask my boss." | Possible. Or a polite exit. | "What would your boss need to see to say yes?" |
| "Can you send me a deck?" | They're trying to end the call. | "Happy to. What's the decision process on your side?" |
| "This would save us so much time." | Still not commitment. | "Would you be willing to run a pilot to measure that?" |
| **"When can we start?"** | **Buy signal. Close immediately.** | **"I can have you set up by Tuesday. Does that work?"** |
| **"I want this for [other project/team]."** | **Champion behavior. They're selling internally.** | **"Who should I talk to there? Can you intro me?"** |

## THE FIRST 5 MEETINGS: Special Rules

From Dorian Barker (2025) and First Round PMF Method:

### Rule 1: No Slides
For your first 5 customer meetings, **ban slides**. Force yourself to have a genuine conversation. Slides make you pitch. Conversations make you learn.

### Rule 2: One Motion Goal Per Week
> "Sell to one design partner this week."  
Not: "Build a perfect pitch deck." Not: "Create a partner program."  
Just: Get one commitment.

### Rule 3: Document Everything
After 5 meetings, you should have:
- A list of the exact words customers use to describe their pain
- 3–5 common objection patterns
- Your first case study (even if it's just a pilot agreement)
- A rough pricing hypothesis

### Rule 4: Interview Strangers, Not Friends
Friends and family will lie to protect your feelings. Only count feedback from strangers who match your ICP.

### Rule 5: Weight "No" More Than "Yes"
If 3 of 20 people love your idea and 17 are lukewarm — the 17 are your market. Build for them.

## HANDLING OBJECTIONS

### "We already have Autodesk Construction Cloud / Procore"

> "ACC is great for model coordination. But when your superintendent is on site and needs to know the concrete strength — they're not opening Revit. They're calling the office. Medha reads the documents ACC doesn't touch — the specs, the RFIs, the contracts — and answers in plain English. It complements ACC, doesn't replace it."

### "We can just Ctrl+F through PDFs"

> "Fair. How long did your last Ctrl+F search take? And did you find the answer in the first document, or the 15th? Medha reads all 10,000 pages simultaneously. The 20 minutes your PM spends searching? That's 20 minutes not managing subcontractors."

### "We're concerned about uploading documents to the cloud"

> "Totally understand. That's why we built the 3-factor authentication mesh — users have to prove project knowledge before they get access. Plus, we can deploy on-premise if your IT requires it. Would a private deployment address that concern?"

### "This seems expensive"

> "What does one RFI delay cost you? Or one concrete pour with wrong specs? Our enterprise clients tell us one caught contradiction pays for a year of Medha. Would a pilot let us measure the ROI on your actual project?"

### "We don't have time to implement another tool"

> "That's exactly why we built this. No integration. No training. You upload documents. We process them. Your team asks questions. That's it. Setup takes 30 minutes. Pilot takes 30 days. If it doesn't save 5 hours in month one, we part as friends."

## MEETING TYPES & ADAPTATIONS

### Coffee Chat (Informal, 20 min)
- Skip the formal demo
- Focus entirely on discovery
- Ask: "What's the most annoying part of your job that nobody talks about?"
- End with: "Can I show you something quick next week? 10 minutes."

### Video Call (Formal, 30 min)
- Full 4-phase flow
- Share screen for demo
- Record with permission (for internal review)

### Site Visit (High-value, 60 min)
- Walk the job site first
- Ask them to show you where document pain happens
- Demo on a tablet in the site trailer
- Highest conversion rate — they see it in THEIR environment

### Group Demo (Committee, 45 min)
- Start with the executive summary: "X hours saved, Y contradictions caught"
- Then do the 3-minute demo
- End with ROI calculation
- Leave behind: one-page case study + pilot proposal

## THE "WHY QUESTIONS" CHEAT SHEET

Print this. Keep it visible during every meeting.

### The 5 Whys for Customer Discovery

When a prospect mentions a pain, keep asking why:

1. **"Why is that a problem?"**
2. **"Why does that keep happening?"**
3. **"Why haven't you fixed it yet?"**
4. **"Why would solving that matter to your boss?"**
5. **"Why is NOW the right time to address it?"**

### The Switch Interview (JTBD)

When they DID solve a similar problem (or tried to):
- "What made you start looking for a solution?"
- "What pushed you from 'this is annoying' to 'I need to fix this'?"
- "What did you try before? Why didn't it work?"
- "What would make you switch from your current approach?"

### The Timeline Probe

- "When did this problem start?"
- "What changed to make it worse?"
- "What happens if you don't solve it in the next 6 months?"
- "What would you do if we weren't having this conversation?"
