PROJECT: HIGHRISE TOWER - PHASE 2
FIRE PROTECTION SPECIFICATIONS
SECTION 21 13 00 - FIRE SUPPRESSION SPRINKLERS

1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
   1.1 All fire protection work shall conform to NFPA 13 (2019 Edition).
   1.2 All work shall be performed by a licensed fire protection contractor.
   1.3 Hydraulic calculations required for all systems.
   1.4 Water supply: 8-inch dedicated fire service from city main.

2. SPRINKLER SYSTEM DESIGN
   2.1 System type: Wet pipe throughout, except dry pipe in unheated areas.
   2.2 Design density: 0.15 gpm/sf for light hazard areas.
   2.3 Design density: 0.20 gpm/sf for ordinary hazard Group 1 areas.
   2.4 Design density: 0.30 gpm/sf for ordinary hazard Group 2 areas.
   2.5 Maximum protected area per sprinkler head: 225 square feet.
   2.6 Sprinkler spacing: maximum 15 feet on center.
   2.7 Sprinkler spacing from walls: maximum 7.5 feet.
   2.8 Minimum operating pressure: 7 psi at most remote head.

3. SPRINKLER HEAD REQUIREMENTS
   3.1 Office areas: quick-response, pendent, 155 degrees Fahrenheit rated.
   3.2 Corridors: quick-response, sidewall, 155 degrees Fahrenheit rated.
   3.3 Mechanical rooms: standard response, upright, 175 degrees Fahrenheit rated.
   3.4 Parking garage: standard response, upright, 200 degrees Fahrenheit rated.
   3.5 All sprinkler heads: chrome finish unless noted otherwise.

4. PIPE AND FITTINGS
   4.1 Above-ground pipe: Schedule 40 steel, threaded or grooved.
   4.2 Fittings: malleable iron threaded, 300 psi rated.
   4.3 Hangers: adjustable swivel ring, spaced per NFPA 13 table.
   4.4 Pipe size: minimum 1-inch for branch lines, 6-inch for main risers.
   4.5 Pipe clearance to structure: 6 inches minimum from all beams and decks.

5. FIRE PUMP
   5.1 Fire pump: electric-driven, 1,000 gpm rated capacity.
   5.2 Fire pump pressure: 125 psi rated at churn.
   5.3 Jockey pump: 15 gpm, maintains system pressure.
   5.4 Controller: full service, with manual start and automatic transfer.

6. STANDPIPES
   6.1 Class I standpipe: 2.5-inch outlet, one per floor at stairwell.
   6.2 Standpipe pressure: 100 psi residual at topmost outlet.
   6.3 Flow rate: 500 gpm for first standpipe, 250 gpm for each additional.

SECTION 08 71 00 - FIRE-RATED DOOR ASSEMBLIES

1. GENERAL
   1.1 All fire-rated doors shall conform to NFPA 80.
   1.2 All doors shall be listed and labeled by a recognized testing agency.
   1.3 Hardware shall be fire-rated and compliant with ADA requirements.

2. DOOR RATINGS
   2.1 Stairwell doors: 90-minute fire rating, 1.5-hour label.
   2.2 Corridor doors: 45-minute fire rating, 3/4-hour label.
   2.3 Mechanical room doors: 90-minute fire rating, 1.5-hour label.
   2.4 Electrical room doors: 60-minute fire rating, 1-hour label.
   2.5 Tenant separation doors: 45-minute fire rating, 3/4-hour label.

3. DOOR MATERIALS
   3.1 Hollow metal doors: 18-gauge steel face, mineral core.
   3.2 Vision panels: 1/4-inch wired glass in steel frame.
   3.3 Frames: 16-gauge steel, welded corners.
   3.4 Seals: intumescent seals at head and jambs.

4. HARDWARE
   4.1 Closers: heavy-duty, adjustable, 180-degree swing.
   4.2 Locks: fail-safe electrified mortise, keyed to master system.
   4.3 Panic hardware: touchpad style on all egress doors.
