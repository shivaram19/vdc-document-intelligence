PROJECT: HIGHRISE TOWER - PHASE 2
STRUCTURAL SPECIFICATIONS
SECTION 03 30 00 - CAST-IN-PLACE CONCRETE

1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
   1.1 All concrete work shall conform to ACI 318-19 Building Code Requirements for Structural Concrete.
   1.2 Concrete shall be supplied by a certified ready-mix supplier.
   1.3 All pours require 48-hour advance notice to the structural engineer.

2. CONCRETE STRENGTH REQUIREMENTS
   2.1 Column concrete strength: 6,000 psi at 28 days (f'c = 6,000 psi).
   2.2 Beam concrete strength: 5,000 psi at 28 days (f'c = 5,000 psi).
   2.3 Slab-on-grade concrete strength: 4,000 psi at 28 days (f'c = 4,000 psi).
   2.4 Elevated slab concrete strength: 5,000 psi at 28 days (f'c = 5,000 psi).
   2.5 Foundation mat concrete strength: 6,000 psi at 28 days (f'c = 6,000 psi).

3. SLAB THICKNESS REQUIREMENTS
   3.1 Typical floor slab thickness: 8 inches minimum.
   3.2 Transfer slab thickness: 14 inches minimum.
   3.3 Slab-on-grade thickness: 6 inches minimum.
   3.4 Roof slab thickness: 10 inches minimum.
   3.5 All slab thicknesses subject to structural engineer review and approval.

4. REINFORCEMENT REQUIREMENTS
   4.1 Column vertical bars: #11 grade 60 rebar, spacing not exceeding 12 inches.
   4.2 Beam stirrups: #4 grade 60 rebar, spacing 6 inches on center.
   4.3 Slab reinforcing: #5 grade 60 rebar at 12 inches on center both ways.
   4.4 Temperature reinforcement: #4 grade 60 rebar at 18 inches on center.
   4.5 All rebar shall be epoxy-coated where exposed to moisture.

5. FORMWORK REQUIREMENTS
   5.1 Formwork shall be designed for full hydrostatic pressure plus 25 psf live load.
   5.2 Deflection limit: L/360 for visible surfaces, L/480 for architectural finishes.
   5.3 Form release agent shall be non-staining and VOC-compliant.

6. CURING REQUIREMENTS
   6.1 Minimum curing period: 7 days for all structural elements.
   6.2 Curing compound: ASTM C309 Type 1, Class A.
   6.3 Temperature monitoring required for all pours exceeding 100 cubic yards.

7. QUALITY CONTROL
   7.1 Test cylinders: 1 set per 100 cubic yards or per pour, whichever is more frequent.
   7.2 Slump test: Every truck, must be between 3 and 6 inches.
   7.3 Air content: 4% to 7% for all exterior slabs.
   7.4 Core testing required if any cylinder fails below 90% of specified strength.

SECTION 05 12 00 - STRUCTURAL STEEL

1. GENERAL
   1.1 All structural steel shall conform to ASTM A992 Grade 50.
   1.2 Welding shall conform to AWS D1.1.
   1.3 All connections require shop drawings approved by the engineer of record.

2. STEEL MEMBERS
   2.1 Wide flange beams: W24x55 minimum for typical floors.
   2.2 Columns: W14x90 minimum for typical columns.
   2.3 Transfer girders: W36x160 minimum.
   2.4 All steel shall be primed with SSPC-SP6 commercial blast cleaning.

3. FIREPROOFING
   3.1 Spray-applied fire-resistive material (SFRM) required on all steel members.
   3.2 Fire rating: 2-hour rating for all primary structural members.
   3.3 SFRM thickness: 1.5 inches minimum on beams, 2.0 inches on columns.
