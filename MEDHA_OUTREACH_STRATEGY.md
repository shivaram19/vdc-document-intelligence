## 1. THE "WHY" FRAMEWORK: Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

> *"Customers don't buy products. They hire them to get jobs done."* — Clayton Christensen

Traditional construction tech marketing focuses on **features** (AI, RAG, embeddings). JTBD research (DriveResearch 2025, Directive Consulting 2026) proves this fails. Buyers don't care about your tech stack. They care about **outcomes**.

### The 5 Core Jobs Medha Is Hired For

| # | Job Statement | Functional | Emotional | Social |
|---|--------------|------------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **"Find the spec clause I need before the concrete truck arrives"** | Retrieve document facts in <10 seconds | Feel confident, not frantic | Look prepared to the superintendent |
| 2 | **"Catch contradictions before they cost me $50K in rework"** | Scan specs vs. drawings automatically | Sleep better, reduce anxiety | Protect my reputation as a careful PM |
| 3 | **"Draft RFIs that architects actually answer quickly"** | Auto-reference source documents | Feel competent, not annoying | Be seen as professional, not clueless |
| 4 | **"Onboard new engineers without losing tribal knowledge"** | Searchable project memory | Feel like a good mentor/leader | Build a legacy, not just projects |
| 5 | **"Prove to the owner we checked everything"** | Generate audit trails + evidence | Feel defensible in disputes | Demonstrate professionalism for future bids |

### The "Why Questions" You Must Answer in Every Touchpoint

Before writing a single email or landing page, answer these JTBD research questions (DriveResearch 2025, CleverX 2026):

1. **"What was the last time you wasted 2+ hours searching for a spec clause? What were you doing right before?"**
2. **"When a contradiction caused rework on your last project, who got blamed? How did that feel?"**
3. **"What do you currently do when a new engineer asks 'what's the concrete strength for this project'?"**
4. **"If you could have one superpower on your job site, what would it be?"** (The answer is almost always "know everything instantly")
5. **"Walk me through the last RFI you wrote. How long did it take? Did you get the answer you needed?"**

> **Key Insight:** 80% of product features are rarely or never used (Pendo 2025). Medha's 3-factor auth and agent mesh are **product differentiators**, but the **marketing message** must focus on the 5 jobs above.

### A. LinkedIn Outreach (Highest ROI for AEC)

**2026 LinkedIn Best Practices** (Donemaker 2025, LinkedIn Year in Review):
- Quality > quantity: 3 thoughtful comments/week > 10 posts/day
- Video carousels + case studies drive 2x comment volume
- Polls to gather pain points, then follow with solution posts
- Warm engagement before DM: comment 3x, then connect, then message

**LinkedIn Sequence for Tier 1 India:**
```
Day 1: Comment on prospect's post about project challenges
Day 3: Comment again + share relevant industry stat
Day 5: Connection request with note: "Saw your work on [project]. Curious how you handle spec lookups on site?"
Day 8: DM: "Quick question — do your PMs ever waste time searching for clauses during pours? We built something for that."
Day 14: Share case study (not pitch)
Day 21: Soft pitch: "Happy to show you how [similar firm] cut RFI time by 60%"
```

**LinkedIn Content Calendar (Weekly):**
- Monday: Industry stat + "What we see" observation
- Wednesday: 60-second video: "Here's a contradiction we found in a real project"
- Friday: Case study carousel: "Before/After: How [Client] eliminated duplicate RFIs"
